Predictive Intrusion Detection System Using Network Behavioral Analysis and Machine Learning

Member 1 Mohamed Soliman
Member 2 Ahmed Mahmoud
Member 3 Ahmed Mubarak
Member 4 Yehia Allam
Member 5 Gannah Mohamed
Abstract

Most contemporary cybersecurity systems operate reactively, detecting breaches only after malicious activity has commenced. This research proposes "Sentinel IDS", a proactive Intrusion Detection System designed to forecast cyber attacks before they execute. By extracting and analyzing network traffic flows using Sliding Windows, the system leverages Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks combined with an XGBoost baseline to predict attacks with an average prediction horizon of 42.5 seconds. Furthermore, the framework integrates Explainable AI (SHAP) to ensure algorithmic transparency, empowering SOC analysts to understand and trust the predictive alerts.
